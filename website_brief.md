> **Purpose:** Everything a web designer/developer needs to build the VeraOps marketing site.

### Brand Voice
- **Tone:** Confident, warm, direct. Speaks like a fellow production owner who figured something out — not a SaaS vendor. No corporate BS.
- **Personality:** Smart but approachable. Data-driven but human. Premium but not pretentious. Understands the feast-or-famine reality.
- **Avoid:** Corporate jargon, hype words ("revolutionary", "game-changing"), cold/robotic language, anything that sounds like it was written by someone who's never been on set.
- **Emulate:** The clarity of Superhuman's copy, the warmth of a production company's sizzle reel voice-over, the sophistication of a boutique consultancy.

### Visual Identity (from current site)
- **Primary color:** Light green/cream (#f3f8ea)

- **Accent:** Blue (#09f) for links/CTAs
- **Font:** Inter (weights 400–900)
- **Style:** Minimalist, generous whitespace, card-based layouts, 24px border radius on cards
- **Feel:** Premium SaaS meets executive briefing. Clean, not cluttered.

## 2. TARGET AUDIENCE

### Primary (and only): Video Production Agency Owners

- Founders and owners of video production agencies (not video marketing agencies, not creative agencies broadly — video production specifically)
- Typically led by creatives: founders who are also producers, directors, or creative directors
- **Company profile:** 3–30 years in business, $20K–$150K average project values, team size 5–50
- Have 500–2,000+ business contacts accumulated over years of shoots, projects, and industry events — creative directors at agencies, brand-side marketing leads, fellow producers, agency account people
- Referral-dependent: most new work comes from people who already know their work, but referrals are unpredictable
- Feast-or-famine revenue cycles tied to project timelines — when shoots pile up, biz dev stops completely
- Know their network is valuable but have zero system for maintaining it between projects
- Growth is reactive, not strategic — no consistent outreach infrastructure
- Guilt about letting relationships go cold after a project wraps

A: We use Google OAuth2 — the same secure method used by Superhuman, Mixmax, and other email tools. We read email metadata and threads to understand your relationships. We never send emails on your behalf without your explicit action. Your data is encrypted at rest and in transit.

**Q: Is my data shared with anyone?**
A: Never. Your contact data, email content, and relationship analysis are completely private to your account. We don't sell data. We don't train AI models on your emails.

**Q: How long does setup take?**
A: About 5 minutes. Connect your Gmail and Calendar via OAuth, and we begin analyzing your production network immediately. Your first digest arrives the next business day. Full network tiering completes within 24–48 hours depending on contact volume.

**Q: What if a suggested contact's email bounces?**
A: We verify every email before it reaches your digest using FindyMail, a professional email verification service. We check against LinkedIn profiles and company domains. If we can't verify a high-confidence email, the contact won't appear in your digest.

**Q: Can I customize what contacts are suggested?**
A: Yes. You can set quarterly goals, target contact profiles (agency type, seniority, company size), outreach frequency preferences, and flag contacts as do-not-contact. The AI adapts to your preferences over time.

**Q: What happens if I don't act on a suggestion?**
A: Nothing bad. After 7 days without a click, the suggestion is marked as "ignored" and the contact re-enters the rotation pool. No pressure, no guilt. The system is designed around your pace — especially when you're heads-down on a shoot.

**Q: How is this different from a CRM?**
A: CRMs track deals. VeraOps tracks relationships. A CRM tells you a project closed 18 months ago. VeraOps tells you that the creative director who brought you that project just moved to a bigger agency, has been posting about needing production partners, and here's a draft email to congratulate them on the move. CRMs are record-keeping. VeraOps is a living system — constantly refreshing signals, updating contact intelligence, and surfacing new opportunities. It's not a static database. It's always working, even when you're on set.

**Q: How is this different from outbound agencies like Surge?**
A: They find new people. We resurface people who already know your work. Surge and similar agencies are great at generating cold conversations with people who've never heard of you. VeraOps is built for the other side — warm conversations with creative directors who've already sat in your edit suite, agency contacts who've already seen your reels, and brand-side decision-makers who already know the quality of your production work. One gets you cold meetings. The other gets you warm conversations that close faster and at higher project values. They're actually complementary — Surge for new, VeraOps for existing.

**Q: Do you support multiple team members?**
A: Yes. Each user gets their own digest calibrated to their voice and goals. Great for production companies where the founder and an EP or head of sales each manage their own relationships.

## 5. HOW IT WORKS PAGE

### Hero
**Headline:** "From 1,200 production contacts to 3 perfect suggestions. Here's the journey."

### The Pipeline (visual flowchart)

**Phase 1 — Extraction (One-time setup)**
"We connect to your Gmail and Calendar and analyze every business conversation you've ever had. For each contact, we extract:"
- Relationship narrative (how you connected, what you've produced together)
- Conversation topics (branded content, commercial, corporate, events)
- Unfinished threads (conversations left hanging after a project wrapped)
- Favors & referrals (who referred whom, who made introductions)
- Initiation pattern (who usually reaches out first)
- Tone classification (formal, warm, casual, collaborative)
- Strategic value (potential client, referral source, agency partner, overflow partner)
- Seniority level (coordinator through C-suite/founder)

"This happens once. For a production network of 1,200 contacts, it takes about 24 hours. Then it's always up to date."

**Phase 2 — Prioritization**
"We tier your entire network using AI:"
- **Tier A (100 contacts):** Your hottest reactivation candidates — creative directors, agency leads, and brand contacts most likely to have production work. Checked for signals daily.
- **Tier B (200 contacts):** Warm contacts. Checked weekly. Backup when Tier A is exhausted.
- **Tier C (500 contacts):** Decent contacts. Checked bi-weekly. Can be promoted when signals fire.
- **Tier D (unlimited):** Everyone else. Monthly maintenance.
- **Tier E:** Do-not-contact. Automatically flagged by AI (purely transactional, one-off vendor relationships).

"Tiers rotate automatically. When a Tier A contact is suggested, they move to Tier B. The best Tier B contact gets promoted. Your pipeline never goes stale."

**Phase 3 — Signal Detection**
"Every day, we scan LinkedIn and Google News for your Tier A contacts:"
- Job changes (creative director moved to a new agency in last 90 days)
- Promotions (title upgrades, especially to director/VP/partner level)
- New ventures (contact started their own agency or production company)
- Recent LinkedIn posts (only if topic-relevant to your production capabilities)
- Company wins (agency won a new account with big content needs)
- Product and service launches (new products = new content needs)
- Executive changes at their company (new CMO = new production relationships to build)

"Signals create urgency. A creative director moving agencies is a 90-day window. A company winning a new account means they're sourcing production partners. A relevant post means they're thinking about something you can help with."

**Phase 4 — Recommendation**
"Our AI evaluates your top 30 eligible contacts and selects 3 based on:"
- Strongest 'why today' angle (trigger signal, unfinished thread, goal alignment)
- Time since last suggestion (minimum 30-day gap)
- Your quarterly goals and target profile
- Relationship quality and project history

"For each selected contact, it generates a draft email — matching your writing style from your email history. Subject line included. Edit it, make it yours, and send."

**Phase 5 — Verification & Delivery**
"Before sending the digest, we verify each contact's work email via FindyMail (LinkedIn-first lookup, then name+domain fallback). Only verified contacts make the cut."

"Your digest arrives every weekday morning, ready for your routine."

**Phase 6 — Outcome Tracking**
"After you receive the digest:"
- Click tracking (real-time via Postmark webhooks)
- Gmail re-scan (weekly — did you email them? did they reply?)
- Calendar scan (did you meet?)
- Automatic bounce detection
- No manual CRM updates needed — everything is tracked for you

"Outcomes only upgrade, never downgrade. 'Clicked' can become 'replied', but 'replied' never goes back to 'clicked'."
